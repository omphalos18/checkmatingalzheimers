"I have witnessed the profound impact of Alzheimer's on the patient and the family. With this project, I hoped to help others in my community." — Caton Tsao, Co-founder · Checkmating Alzheimer's

A personal story turned into community action.

When Caton Tsao's grandparents developed Alzheimer's disease and could no longer recognize him, he felt powerless. He and expert-level chess player Allen Hong decided to fight back with what they knew — chess.

What started as two students hosting local tournaments has grown into a multi-city initiative with a formal research partnership at Clemson University, investigating whether chess can scientifically reduce cognitive decline.
